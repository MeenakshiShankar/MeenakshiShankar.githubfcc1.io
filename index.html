SUSHMA SWARAJ

The Uncomprimising Leader

Here's a time line of Smt. Sushma Swaraj

  • February 14, 1952-born in a middle-class family on February 14,
    1952 in Ambala Cantt, Haryana.
    Her father, Hardev Sharma was a prominent member of the Rashtriya Swayamsewak Sangh (RSS).
    Her mother's name was Laxmi Devi.
  • 1969 - Sushma Swaraj received her early education in Ambala
    and completed her graduation from SD College in Ambala Cantt.
    She was a meritorious student, bagged the gold medal besides
    winning the 'Best Student' award in 1969-70 at her college.
    Sushma Swaraj was also an active cadet of the National Cadet Corps (NCC).
    She was adjudged the best NCC cadet of
    SD College for three consecutive years. She completed her LLB
    from Panjab University in Chandigarh.
  • 1973 - After attaining a law degree from Panjab University,
    Sushma Swaraj started her career as a lawyer in the Supreme Court in 1973.
    This was also the time she started taking part in student politics
    under the banner of Akhil Bharatiya Vidyarthi Parishad (ABVP), the student wing of RSS.
  • 1975 -Sushma Swaraj took active part in the protests against
    the Emergency imposed by then PM Indira Gandhi. As a lawyer,
    she became part of the legal team of social leader George Fernandes.
    It was during these days that she met Swaraj Kaushal, a fellow lawyer whom she married in 1975.
  • 1977 - When Emergency ended in 1977, Sushma Swaraj made history
    by becoming the youngest cabinet minister in any state in India.
    She was just 25 when she became Haryana's Labour and Employment minister.
  • 1979 - Sushma Swaraj became the state president
    of the Janata Party in Haryana at the age of 27.
  • 1987 -Sushma Swaraj was re-elected to the Haryana state assembly
    and served as the minister for education and food and civil supplies.
  • 1990 - 1990 marked Sushma Swaraj's entry into national politics
    she was elected to the Rajya Sabha. As a Rajya Sabha MP, she became chairperson
    of the Joint Committee of Catering
    Committee on Petitions besides being a member of the Committee on Government Assurances.
  • 1996 - The general election of 1996 was another turning
    point in Sushma Swaraj's political career. She was
    elected to the 11th Lok Sabha from the South Delhi seat.
    This was her first stint as a Lok Sabha MP.

    Atal Bihari Vajpayee made Sushma Swaraj a cabinet minister in his government.
    She was made the minister for information
    and broadcasting. However, the Vajpayee government did not last more than 13 days as
    it failed to prove majority in the Lok Sabha.
  • 1998 - Sushma Swaraj won the 1998 Lok Sabha election from South Delhi seat.
    This time as well Atal Bihari Vajpayee included her in his cabinet.
    made the minister for information and broadcasting and was given
    additional charge of telecommunications.
  • October 1998-Sushma Swaraj resigned as a cabinet minister and became
    the BJP's first woman Chief Minister in Delhi. She however served only for 52 days.
    The Congress won the Delhi assembly election with Shiela Dikshit becoming chief minister.
  • 1999 -The BJP fielded Sushma Swaraj to take on Congress's Sonia Gandhi
    from Bellary during the 1999 Lok Sabha elections. Swaraj lost the election.
    Later in 2004 when Congress won the Lok Sabha election, she threatened to don a white saree,
    shave her head, sleep on the floor and eat only chickpeas if Sonia Gandhi
    were to be sworn in as the Prime Minister of India.
  • 2000 - With BJP's defeat in Delhi, Sushma Swaraj returned to
    national politics as she was re-elected to the Rajya Sabha.
    She was again made the minister for information and broadcasting in the Vajpayee government.
  • 2003 - In 2003, Atal Bihari Vajpayee gave
    Sushma Swaraj the portfolios of health and family welfare and
    also made her the parliamentary affairs minister.
  • 2006-The BJP had lost the 2004 Lok Sabha election and was now in the Opposition.
    Sushma Swaraj completed her six-year tenure as Rajya Sabha MP and
    was re-elected to the House in 2006 and was part of important parliamentary committees.
  • 2009-If 1990 marked Sushma Swaraj's entry into national politics,
    the year 2009 saw her meteoric rise. She won the 2009 Lok Sabha election from the
    Vidisha seat and became the Leader of Opposition in the Lok Sabha.
    She was the second woman after Sonia Gandhi to hold this high post.
  • 2014-Sushma Swaraj was elected to the 16th Lok Sabha.
    Being a senior BJP leader, she became the external affairs minister in
    the first Narendra Modi government. She was the first woman to become a full-time
    external affairs minister of India.
  • 2015-This was perhaps the only year
    when Sushma Swaraj's credibility was at stake.
    She was accused of malpractice alleging that she used her influence to arrange travel
    documents for fugitive businessman Lalit Modi who was wanted by the Enforcement
    Directorate over his role in a Rs 425-crore scam.
  • 2019-In the run-up to the 2019 Lok Sabha elections,
    Sushma Swaraj announced that she would not be contesting owing to poor health. In the past few years,
    she had undergone operations and take multiple treatments.
  • August 6, 2019-Sushma Swaraj breathed her last in New Delhi.
    She died after suffering a cardiac arrest. Tributes from leaders across
    political parties, celebrities from all walks of life and the common people
    flooded social media as an inspiring chapter of Indian politics ended.
